\begin{abstract}
	We present a large image dataset collected from Nao V6 humanoid robots in RoboCup soccer environments.
	It contains 1,864,394 images from multiple teams, venues, and years, manually categorized by environmental conditions such as lighting, shadows and reflections, line quality, and field color.
	A representative subset of 601 images is additionally annotated with pixel-wise segmentation masks for six semantic classes and uncertain pixels.
	By combining scale, semantic segmentation labels, and manually assigned condition categories, the dataset enables targeted training and evaluation of robotic vision methods under realistic and challenging RoboCup conditions.

  \url{https://hulks.de/nao-image-segmentation-dataset/}

	\keywords{Computer Vision \and Image Segmentation \and Dataset \and Humanoid Robots}
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

Data-driven methods require large amounts of diverse, high-quality, and domain-specific data with accurate annotations.
Creating such datasets is time-consuming and costly in both collection and annotation.

In the RoboCup \gls{hsl} and former \gls{spl}, computer vision is essential for object detection, obstacle avoidance, and self-localization in autonomous soccer matches.
The long-term goal of playing against human players by 2050 introduces increasingly challenging field conditions, including varying lighting, inconsistent artificial turf, shadows, reflections, and faded field markings.
These challenges highlight the need for datasets that provide not only images and labels, but also descriptions of the situations of recording conditions.

To address this need, we present a large dataset of images captured by Nao V6 robots across multiple teams and events.
The contribution is twofold: first, the dataset provides nearly two million images from real RoboCup environments, including a manually segmented subset; second, each game is manually categorized into relevant environmental situations, enabling targeted training and evaluation for conditions such as lighting, shadows, line quality, and field color.

\section{Dataset}

The dataset contains 1,864,394 images captured by Nao V6 robots across teams and events.
Of these images, 601 are manually annotated with segmentation masks as described in~\Cref{subsec:annotations}.
Approximately 44\% of the images originate from our own team \textit{HULKs}; the remainder was collected from other competing teams: \textit{B-Human}, \textit{SPQR}, \textit{Nao Devils}, \textit{BerlinUnited}, and \textit{Dutch Nao Team}.
For each game, metadata provides the image format and manually assigned environmental conditions.

\subsection{Overview}

The dataset is organized hierarchically by game, robot, and camera.
Each top-level folder represents a single game and is identified by a unique game ID referencing an entry in the metadata file.
This metadata file contains the year, location, competing teams, and number of robots.
Within each of the 166 game folders, subfolders represent individual robots that participated in that game and contain two camera folders for the top and bottom camera images.

The dataset covers recordings from 2019 to 2025, including competitions such as RoboCup and GermanOpen, as well as team spaces and smaller event settings.
Top camera images are provided at a resolution of 640×480 pixels and make up roughly half of the images.
The bottom camera images are mostly 640×480 pixels, with a small subset at 320×240 pixels.
All images are stored in PNG or JPEG format and use RGB or YCrCb color spaces.

In addition to the technical metadata, each game was manually categorized by environmental factors affecting robot soccer vision.
These condition labels are part of the dataset: they make it possible to select images for specific situations and to evaluate models on challenging subsets.

\subsection{Image Segmentation Masks}\label{subsec:annotations}

The dataset includes 601 manually labeled images with segmentation masks (see \Cref{fig:examplesSegmentation}) for six semantic classes: \textit{field}, \textit{lines}, \textit{ball}, \textit{robots}, \textit{goal}, and \textit{others} (e.g., background or humans), plus an \textit{uncertain} label.
The pixels in the uncertain class include the boundary regions between the other classes, as well as areas that humans cannot clearly distinguish.
The annotation process was aided with the \gls{cvat} and \gls{sam}.

\section{Applications and Use Cases}

The dataset supports a range of applications in robotic vision.
The annotated subset can train and evaluate segmentation models, while the large pool of unlabeled images enables custom annotation strategies and semi- or self-supervised learning.
With nearly 2 million images, the dataset provides sufficient scale for training robust models.
It is grounded in the RoboCup domain with Nao V6 robots, but many aspects generalize to other robot soccer settings and related tasks.

A key advantage is the combination of environmental diversity and manual situation labels.
Because each game is categorized by lighting, shadows/reflections, line conditions, and field conditions, researchers can train models for specific scenarios, compare performance across environmental factors, and construct benchmark splits isolating known failure cases.
These evaluations are difficult to perform from raw images and segmentation masks alone.
Overall, the dataset is a flexible resource for developing and testing vision methods under realistic conditions.

\section{Conclusion}

This dataset provides a comprehensive resource for training and evaluating robotic vision systems under realistic and diverse conditions. The large-scale unlabeled data, representative segmentation masks, and manual situation categories enable supervised and semi-supervised learning, targeted benchmarking, and domain adaptation research.
Future work includes expanding the dataset with additional annotations (e.g., bounding boxes or pose keypoints) and providing baseline results for common robotic vision tasks.
